What is ETWS?

The Earthquake and Tsunami Warning System (ETWS) is part of the 3GPP's Public Warning System (PWS). It is designed to deliver timely emergency alerts, such as warnings about earthquakes or tsunamis, directly to mobile users in an affected area.

Why is ETWS Important?

ETWS provides early warnings to mobile subscribers about natural disasters, reducing the impact of earthquakes and tsunamis on life and property. It's a crucial system for ensuring the safety of citizens in vulnerable regions.

ETWS Message Structure

ETWS messages are broadcast to all users in the affected area. A message typically includes:

  • Warning Type: Identifies the type of emergency (e.g., earthquake, tsunami).
  • Message ID: A unique identifier for the message.
  • Repetition Period: How often the message is repeated.
  • Emergency User Alert: Indicates whether the alert should be presented to the user with an audible sound or vibration.

Example ETWS Message

        Message ID: 4352
        Serial Number: 0001
        Warning Type: Earthquake
        Repetition Period: 1s
        User Alert: ON

Above is an example of a primary ETWS message that could be sent out during an earthquake alert. The repetition period of 1 second ensures that users receive timely warnings in the event of a disaster.
